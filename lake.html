Lakes are bodies of water surrounded by land, formed through natural processes like tectonic activity, glacial movement, or volcanic eruptions. They can be freshwater or saline, depending on the water sources feeding into them. Lakes play a crucial role in local ecosystems, providing habitat for a variety of plant and animal species, and serve as important water sources for humans, agriculture, and recreation. The water in lakes can be still or exhibit movement due to wind, currents, or inflow from rivers or streams. Many lakes, whether large like the Great Lakes or small and secluded, are treasured for their beauty and tranquility, offering opportunities for fishing, boating, and sightseeing.
